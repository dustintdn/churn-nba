# Model Card — SMB Churn + Next-Best-Action Engine

A short, structured summary of the model behind this project, following the spirit
of [Model Cards](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.03993). Metrics below are reproduced
from `models/metrics.json` and refresh whenever `python src/train.py` runs.

## Model details

- **Task:** binary classification — predict whether an SMB customer will churn.
- **Architecture:** scikit-learn `Pipeline` = `ColumnTransformer` (StandardScaler on
  numerics, one-hot on categoricals) → **XGBoost** classifier → **isotonic
  `CalibratedClassifierCV`** wrapper for trustworthy probabilities.
- **Selection:** chosen automatically by held-out PR-AUC over a logistic-regression
  baseline, default XGBoost, and `RandomizedSearchCV`-tuned XGBoost (`src/train.py`).
- **Imbalance handling:** `scale_pos_weight = #neg / #pos` (no resampling).
- **Artifact:** `models/churn_model.joblib` — bundles preprocessing + model so the
  serving layer scores raw records with no train/serve skew.

## Intended use

- **Primary use:** rank a customer book by churn risk and surface a recommended,
  dollar-prioritized retention action per account (see `src/recommend.py`,
  `src/economics.py`).
- **Users:** retention / customer-success teams and the analysts supporting them.
- **Out of scope:** automated, no-human-in-the-loop account actions; pricing or
  credit decisions; any use on individuals (this is a B2B account-level model).

## Training data

- **Source:** *synthetic* dataset generated by `src/generate_data.py` (clearly
  labeled). Churn is driven by a documented relationship with realistic non-linear
  and interaction effects. Chosen for full reproducibility without external credentials.
- **Size:** 6,000 customers; **base churn rate ≈ 16.8%**.
- **Split:** 80/20 stratified train/test (4,800 / 1,200).
- **Features:** tenure, monthly spend, logins/week, days since last login, active
  campaigns, support tickets (90d), discount %, recent-price-increase flag,
  account-manager flag, NPS, contract type, plan tier, payment method.

## Evaluation

Held-out test set (1,200 customers). Metrics emphasize the minority (churn) class.

| Model | PR-AUC | ROC-AUC |
|---|---|---|
| Logistic regression (baseline) | 0.571 | 0.848 |
| XGBoost (default) | 0.568 | 0.834 |
| **XGBoost (tuned) — deployed** | **0.574** | **0.843** |

- **PR-AUC 0.57** vs. a 0.168 base rate ≈ **3.4× lift**.
- **Operating point:** targeting the top 15% of accounts by risk catches ~51% of
  churners at ~57% precision (notebook re-run; small variation across runs).
- **Recovery check:** on the synthetic data the model reaches ROC-AUC 0.842 against
  an oracle ceiling of 0.853 computed from the true data-generating process
  (notebook Phase 7).
- **Calibration:** isotonic; predicted probabilities track observed churn frequency
  (calibration curve in the notebook).

## Ethical considerations & limitations

- **Synthetic data:** absolute metric levels and feature effects will differ on real
  data; the *methodology* is what transfers.
- **NBA cost/lift are assumptions:** the expected-value figures depend on per-action
  cost and retention-lift assumptions in `src/economics.py`. These should be
  validated with holdout A/B experiments before the dollar outputs are trusted.
- **Point-in-time:** the model scores a snapshot. Production use needs monitoring for
  feature/label drift and periodic recalibration.
- **Fairness:** an account-level B2B model on business attributes; no protected
  personal attributes are used. Re-review if deployed on data that includes them.
